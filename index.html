Now if only we could do something about the 20,000 pets killed by Broward County every year...
Over 20,000 shelter pets are euthanized in Broward County each year. In 2013 it cost taxpayers an average of $223 per animal. While Broward spends nearly $5.3 million on the animal services budget, only a fraction of that goes towards programs to end the killing.

Pets’ Broward is a group of compassionate citizens who are committed to ending the yearly killing of thousands of pets in Broward’s public and private shelters through the implementation of the No Kill resolution unanimously passed by the Broward County Commission.
